Daylily description

The daylily Jerry's Girl was hybridized by Elliott-S. in 2014. Jerry's Girl was hybridized by crossing (Two to Tango × Just for Breakfast). The color of Jerry's Girl can be described as coral pink petals with lighter rose sepals of varying intensity above a green throat, first few blooms often single. It is a Diploid. It has a Double 85% form. It has a bud count of 26. It blooms in (Early-Midseason,Rebloom). It's flowers measure (3.87 in, (10 cm)). It has 4 branches. It has a normal scape height of (26 in, (66 cm)). It is Fragrant.
