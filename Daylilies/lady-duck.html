Daylily description

The daylily Lady Duck was hybridized by Eller in 2008. Lady Duck was hybridized by crossing ((J.T. Davis × sdlg) × (Mardi Gras Ball × sdlg)). The color of Lady Duck can be described as pink with rose band and edge outlined ruffled gold above prominent green throat. It is a Tetraploid. It has a bud count of 28. It blooms in (Early,Rebloom). It's flowers measure (5.75 in, (15 cm)). It has 5 branches. It has a normal scape height of (24 in, (61 cm)). It is Fragrant.
